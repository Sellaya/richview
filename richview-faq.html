FAQ for Investors

MIC investing and Richview Capital

A Mortgage Investment Corporation (MIC) is a Canadian investment vehicle that pools capital from investors to fund residential and commercial mortgages. MICs are regulated under the Income Tax Act and offer investors asset-backed returns through mortgage interest income. Richview Capital MIC focuses on residential mortgages and construction financing across Ontario.

Returns vary based on market conditions and portfolio performance. MICs typically provide distributions from mortgage interest income. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Contact our team for current distribution information and historical performance data.

Yes, investments in Richview Capital MIC can be held within registered accounts including RRSPs, RRIFs, and TFSAs, subject to meeting specific regulatory requirements. This allows investors to earn tax-advantaged income from mortgage investments.

Minimum investment amounts vary depending on the investment structure and account type. Contact our investor relations team to discuss minimum investment requirements and available investment options that suit your financial goals.

All mortgages in the MIC portfolio are secured by real estate assets. We employ conservative loan-to-value ratios and rigorous underwriting standards. The diversified portfolio spreads risk across multiple properties and borrowers, providing additional security for investors.

Distribution frequency depends on your investment structure and preferences. Options typically include monthly or quarterly distributions. Our team will work with you to establish a distribution schedule that aligns with your income needs and investment strategy.
